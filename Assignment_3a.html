Choropleth Map


Definition:The hierarchical statistical map method is to divide the grades according to the statistical data of each zoning unit and the density, intensity or development level of the phenomenon, and then fill and draw different colors or longitudes according to the divisions on the map according to the level, so as to show the differences among the zoning units.


When the data contains zoning units(province, states, cities) and continuous statistical data, it is good to use choropleth maps. For instance, population density or per-capita income can be effectively presented by Choropleth map.
